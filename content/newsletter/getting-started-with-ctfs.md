Capture the Flag (CTF) competitions are one of the fastest, most fun ways to learn security. If
you've been curious but intimidated, this guide is for you.

## What is a CTF?

A CTF is a set of security puzzles. Each challenge hides a "flag" — a string like
`flag{y0u_found_me}` — that you recover by solving the puzzle. Find the flag, earn points.

## Common categories

- **Web** — find and exploit bugs in web apps (injection, auth flaws).
- **Crypto** — break weak or misused cryptography.
- **Reversing** — figure out what a compiled binary does.
- **Forensics** — dig through files, memory, or network captures for clues.
- **Pwn** — exploit memory-corruption bugs in programs.

## How to get your first flag

1. **Pick a beginner-friendly platform** — sites like picoCTF are built for newcomers.
2. **Start with "warmup" web or forensics challenges.** They rely more on curiosity than deep
   theory.
3. **Read the writeups afterward.** Learning *how* others solved it is where the growth happens.
4. **Come to our CTF nights!** We solve challenges together — bring your laptop and questions.

## Why it's worth it

CTFs build the exact instincts employers want: creative problem-solving, comfort with unfamiliar
systems, and persistence. Plus, they're genuinely fun — there's nothing like the rush of that
first flag.
